Hi, I’m Aarav. 

I’m a computer science student at the University of California, Berkeley, where I spend most of my time thinking about machine learning systems and large language models.

I'm interested in how we can transform large language models from text generators into systems that reason, learn strategies, and interact with the world. Much of my current work reflects that. At BAIR (Berkeley's AI Research lab) my team and I have been been working on a reinforcement learning pipeline that teaches LLMs to play poker that exploits human biases. I’m also working as a machine learning research intern at ExodusPoint Capital Management, where I'm experimenting with multimodal ML systems to extract insights from unstructured financial data. 

Over the past few years I’ve been lucky to work on a range of interesting projects. At Invisalign, I post-trained language models for domain-specific translation using SFT and DPO, and worked closely with professional translators to align the models with human preference. Earlier, I worked on a voice-activated augmented reality interface for astronauts as part of Berkeley’s NASA SUITS team, which we later presented to engineers at NASA’s Johnson Space Center. 

More generally, I like building systems that combine research ideas with practical engineering. I’m especially drawn to problems where new ML techniques meet real-world applications.

Outside of computer science, I’m a lifelong soccer player and fan (Hala Madrid). Having grown up across four different countries, I also love to travel and meet new people.

If you're working on interesting problems in machine learning or trying to build something ambitious, I'd love to hear from you!
